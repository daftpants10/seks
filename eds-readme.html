Experience Design Studios

EDS

The goal is to collect blueprints for different dancefloors, ways to embody that through movement or the ⦿-⦿, and eventually arrive at a new system for how we think of collective listening spaces.

The Idea
EDS is a system for designing interactive experiences. Our goal is to match artists with rooms, allowing for the listener to feel good.
The 8 EDS blueprints are examples of how artists have imagined different listening spaces.
Each Blueprint Contains
Every EDS page follows the same structure.
01
Cheatcode
The underlying principle, mapped 1 to 8.
02
Room
A one-liner description of the space, in the words of the artist.
03
Reference Set
A set or sound reference that carries the room.
04
Moodboard
Visual references for the space.
05
Notes
Materials, constraints, observations from the artist.
The 8 Blueprints
Studio Room
EDS 01 The Infinity A dark room. One source of light. Something changes when you enter.
EDS 02 Navigate the Game Five zones. Each activates one sense. Light, tone, texture, scent, taste.
EDS 03 The Code A room that responds to movement history. The longer you stay, the more it reveals.
EDS 04 The Access Point A room designed to produce awe. Immersive ceiling, a horizon, a threshold.
EDS 05 Bleed-Through A space of mirrors and echoes. What you do gets reflected, with a delay.
EDS 06 Find Your Tribe The collective signal is louder than the individual signal.
EDS 07 Build Pyramids Each step inward narrows the space and deepens the experience.
EDS 08 The Lotus A room that only works when many people are in it together. Alone, nothing happens.

The Questionnaire
Based on deep listening by Judith Beker, we want to understand listening as a practice of the habitus, as a process that affects the collective space. We do an onboarding where we help worldbuild with the artist what is their dream room and figure out how to build it together.
→
What does the room feel like before the music starts?
→
Who is in the room, and where are they in relation to the sound?
→
What is the first sound they hear walking in?
→
What does collective listening mean in this space?
→
What does the floor feel like underfoot?
→
Which cheatcode are you building toward?
For Commissioners
Each studio has a brief. The guiding question: what deepens what the body is already learning? Art, installation, scent, lighting, floor materials, thresholds. If it changes how the body moves or listens, it belongs.
At seks.design Activations
Each activation runs a curated set of studios. Moving through rooms over a night means moving through body states. The path through the rooms is the arc of the evening.
