Experience Design Studios / System

How to
Use EDS

A worldbuilding system for regenerative dancefloor design. Each studio is a complete unit. Together they form a curriculum that moves through you.

The Idea
EDS is a system for designing experiences that teach movement philosophy through music and space. Wordlessly. The room does the work.
Each of the 8 studios is built around a single cheatcode: a principle from the seks movement practice. The room embodies it. The music transmits it. The body receives it.
At seks.design activations, rooms rotate. Each activation runs a different combination of studios, which changes the movement practice, the crowd dynamic, and what gets commissioned. The activations are the curriculum.
The System
01
Cheatcode
The underlying movement philosophy. A principle for how the body listens, moves, and relates to other bodies. Eight principles. Eight studios.
02
Sonic World
The genre, energy, and texture of music that best carries that principle into the body. Minimal techno for planes of reference. Ambient for texture and balance. Festival sound for scale and sky. Each genre teaches something specific.
03
Room Design
The physical space built to make the principle unavoidable. Architecture that enforces the lesson. Where is the booth? What does the floor feel like? Who has sightlines to what? The room is the instrument.
04
DJ / Artist Curation
An artist selects a studio that resonates and curates a set to that cheatcode. A listening practice disguised as a set. The artist's answers to the questionnaire directly shape how the room is built around them.
05
Commissioned Experience
Art, installation, and multisensory work commissioned to extend the principle. Each studio has a brief. The brief tells collaborators: what is the body learning here, and how does your work deepen that?
06
The Activation
A seks.design activation runs a curated set of studios. The combination is the programme. Oscillating through rooms over the course of a night means moving through different body states, different listening modes, different crowd densities and energies. You leave having learned something you can't name.
The 8 Studios
EDS 01
The Infinity
minimal techno. planes of reference, center booth, elevation options, movement-reactive visuals
EDS 02
The Senses
ambient / soundsystem. basement, carpet and wood, DJ faces the wall, mirrors reveal the floor
EDS 03
The Code
organic / eclectic. loft space, plants, small objects for isolation practice, something for every body
EDS 04
The Here
classic club. you are present, the DJ is present, the crowd is the architecture
EDS 05
The Pattern
outdoor festival. sky above, earth below, 4-point surround, power minimal, moves you whether you want it to or not
EDS 06
The Formation
bathroom. transitional space, genre-blending, dissociated, music overheard not intended
EDS 07
The Transformation
tbd. the room where structured things dissolve. pyramid becomes whole.
EDS 08
The Lotus
festival / club hybrid. cosy, minimal, art car maybe, communities overlap, everyone has a direction
For DJs + Sound Artists
EDS is a framework for artists who want their sets to mean something beyond the tracklist. Know which room you're building, and let the space do the rest.
Pick the studio whose principle resonates. Build your set as if the music itself is the movement instruction. Answer the soundsystem questions. Your answers shape how we build the room around you, and who we invite into it.

For Commissioners + Collaborators
Each studio has a brief. The brief tells you: what is the body learning in this room, what is the sonic world, who is the crowd, and what does the space feel like.
We commission work that extends the principle. The guiding question: what deepens what the body is already learning?
Art, installation, scent, lighting design, floor materials, objects, thresholds, transitions between rooms. All of it is commissioning territory. If it changes how the body moves or listens, it belongs in the brief.
At seks.design Activations
Each activation runs a curated set of studios. The combination is the programme.
Moving through rooms over the course of a night means moving through body states: from minimal and referential to ambient and textural to open-sky and scale. Each transition is designed. The path through the rooms is the arc of the evening.
The graphic is the floor plan. The floor plan is the principle. The principle is the music. It's all one thing, and the activation is just one version of it running live.
