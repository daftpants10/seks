EDS 5 : Bleed-Through
"Yawning, sneezing, or hiccups can be seen as signs that the levels of the game are bleeding through into 3D. It can manifest as synchronicities, coincidences, or absurdities."

Bleed-Through

The Stick — Movement Practice
Pick up two sticks — one in each hand. Try to control a single imaginary plane with both of them simultaneously. Let one hand lead. Then the other. Then neither. Then both. Feel the plane between them: not physical, but real. The two sticks are the above and the below. The plane is the bleed-through.
Two sticks. One plane. As above, so below. The loob — the figure-8 of time — lives in this movement. The two circles of the double arrow are the two realities you are always playing from. The plane between them is where the game bleeds through.
The Room
A space of mirrors and echoes. What you do gets reflected with a delay — but the reflection is always in parallel somewhere. The room shows the bleed-through as visual data.
The Principle in Action
As above, so below. As you do something, so you do everything. The fractal nature of your gameplay means every micro-action is a macro-pattern. The yawn is not a yawn — it is a signal.
