track 01 / art installation

the 8 at tryp expo

arvy wears a biometric sensor that streams heart rate and movement data in real time. you hold a stick or a bodyloop and use it to manipulate arvy's limbs with slow, rhythmic pressure. the goal is co-regulation: guiding arvy's nervous system into the fractal adaptive zone.

a large screen shows arvy's DFA alpha 1 signal live. when the value reaches the adaptive range (0.75 to 1.0), the music plays. outside that range, the music pauses. the group's collective touch determines whether the music continues.

track 02 / science study

longitudinal research / fu berlin

the same setup runs as a 14-day longitudinal study through the Mind, Brain, and Cognitive Neuroscience program at Freie Universitat Berlin.

the question: do trained participants guide arvy into the adaptive zone faster, more smoothly, and with greater consistency over time? each session logs DFA alpha 1 history, time in each zone, and glow percentage for later analysis.

what is DFA alpha 1

DFA alpha 1 is the short-range scaling exponent of detrended fluctuation analysis. it measures how self-similar a signal is across time scales, heart beats, or movement cycles.

a value near 1.0 means the signal behaves like pink noise: fractal, adaptive, alive. this is the signature of a healthy, responsive nervous system.

0.75 to 1.0 glow 1.0 to 1.25 flow above 1.5 rigid below 0.5 chaos
0.75 to 1.0 glow / fractal adaptive zone. music plays. this is the sweet spot.
1.0 to 1.25 flow / correlated but trending toward rigidity. sustained activity.
above 1.5 rigid / highly correlated, low variability. fatigued or overcontrolled.
below 0.5 chaos / anti-correlated. erratic, fragmented regulation.

DFA alpha 1 can be computed from RR intervals (cardiac, via Polar H10 or similar chest strap) or from accelerometer magnitude (motion, via Apple Watch + SomaSync). the interpretation is consistent across both signal types.

SomaSync streams at 4 Hz with a 128-sample window, producing a new DFA value every 2 seconds. this gives a responsive, science-grade signal suitable for both art and research.
