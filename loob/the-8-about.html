track 01 / art installation

the 8 at tryp expo

arvy wears a biometric sensor streaming heart rate and movement data in real time. you hold a stick or a bodyloop and use it to manipulate arvy's limbs with slow, rhythmic pressure. the goal is co-regulation: guiding arvy's nervous system into the fractal adaptive zone.

a screen shows the DFA alpha 1 signal live. when the value reaches 0.75 to 1.0, the music plays. the group's collective touch determines whether the music continues.

track 02 / science study

longitudinal research / fu berlin

the same setup runs as a 14-day longitudinal study through the Mind, Brain, and Cognitive Neuroscience program at Freie Universitat Berlin.

the question: do trained participants guide arvy into the adaptive zone faster, more smoothly, and with greater consistency over time? each session logs DFA alpha 1 history, time in each zone, and glow percentage for later analysis.

what is DFA alpha 1

DFA alpha 1 is the short-range scaling exponent of detrended fluctuation analysis. it measures how self-similar a signal is across time scales, heart beats, or movement cycles.

a value near 1.0 means the signal behaves like pink noise: fractal, adaptive, alive. this is the signature of a healthy, responsive nervous system.

0.75 to 1.0 glow 1.0 to 1.25 flow above 1.5 rigid below 0.5 chaos
0.75 to 1.0 glow / fractal adaptive zone. music plays.
1.0 to 1.25 flow / correlated, trending toward rigidity.
above 1.5 rigid / low variability. fatigued or overcontrolled.
below 0.5 chaos / anti-correlated. erratic, fragmented.

DFA alpha 1 can be computed from RR intervals (cardiac, via Polar H10) or from accelerometer magnitude (motion, via Apple Watch + SomaSync). SomaSync streams at 4 Hz with a 128-sample window, updating every 2 seconds.
