Time on the dancefloor doesn't move in a line.
It stretches. It collapses. It circles back.
This is a compass for navigating it.

01 / The Interface

A dot.
A colour wheel.
A map.

The HUD is minimal: one dot on a dark screen, surrounded by a living colour wheel. The dot is you. Its position on the map reflects where you are — in tempo, in mood, in time.

As the music shifts — as the BPM climbs, as the key turns minor, as the era changes — the dot moves. You watch yourself move through music space the way a pilot watches a horizon.

Built for Apple Watch. Works in any browser. The mic listens. The map draws itself.

02 / Time as Space

Time as Space

The map is (not) arbitrary. You give each direction a meaning. From silence to movement, customize your parameters.

03 / The Loop

Track, discover,
export, return.

A session on the Time Compass leaves a record. Not a playlist — a territory. A shape made of where you went, what pulled you, what you found out there.

01

Input

Drop a YouTube link, scan a track, or let the mic identify what's playing. The backend resolves BPM, key, and year — and places it on your map.

02

Discover

As your dot moves, nearby blips appear: other tracks that live in the same region. Same era, similar tempo, related mood. The map suggests where to go next.

03

Export

At the end of the night, export your session as a generative artwork — the path you made, the regions you visited, the music you found. Add the tracks to your library.
