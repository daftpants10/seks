01 / The Interface

A dot.
A colour wheel.
A map.

The HUD is minimal: one dot on a dark screen, surrounded by a living colour wheel. The dot is you. Its position on the map reflects where you are — in tempo, in mood, in time.

As the music shifts — as the BPM climbs, as the key turns minor, as the era changes — the dot moves. You watch yourself move through music space the way a pilot watches a horizon.

Built for Apple Watch. Works in any browser. The mic listens. The map draws itself.

02 / Time as Space

Time as Space

The map is (not) arbitrary. You give each direction a meaning. From silence to movement, set your own parameters.

  • ○○
    Rings
    Any linear metric. Year. Rating 1–10. The 1–12 of a clock. Distance from home. Rings draw the depth of the space.
  • ●
    Colour
    Mapped to sentiment — composite of key, BPM, energy — or to context: weather, location, the moment you Shazamed it.
  • ◉
    Size
    The dot expands and contracts. Heart rate. Intensity. Energy level. You feel it before you read it.
  • ↗
    Orientation
    Direction of travel through the space. Tracers show where you came from and where you're heading.

+ many more. any data a song carries.

03 / The Stick

Your library
is a landscape.
Dig.

Connect your phone or watch to the stick. The stick is the interface. Different movements surface different tracks — not by algorithm, but by the shape of what you've already listened to.

Every track you add gets folded into the map. The stick reads the space around your current position and shows you what lives nearby — similar tempo, same era, related mood. You're not searching. You're digging.
